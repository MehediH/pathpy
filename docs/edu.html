Educational resources

We regularly offer talks, lectures, demonstrations, and tutorials that introduce higher-order network analytics with pathpy. Below we provide information on some of those events, including publicly available educational material and video recordings.

Lecture Series at ETH Zurich

In the spring semester of 2019, Prof. Dr. Frank Schweitzer teaches a lecture on Complex Networks. Through asignment sheets and exercises, students will get an introduction to the modelling of complex networks with pathpy.

Lecture Series at University of Zurich

In the fall semester of 2018, Prof. Dr. Ingo Scholtes offered a series of 12 lectures and exercises on Statistical Network Analysis. In the accompanying exercises, students will get an introduction to network analysis with pathpy.

Hands-on Tutorial at Complexity Science Hub

In September 2018, Ingo Scholtes gave a half-day tutorial on higher-order network analysis with pathpy at the Complexity Science Hub in Vienna, Austria. The material of this tutorial, including slides, data, assignments, and solutions to live coding sessions is available at the tutorial website.

Hands-on Tutorial at KDD'18

In August 2018, we will give a full-day hands-on tutorial on Mining Time Series Data with Higher-order Network Models at the flagship data science conference KDD'19 in London, UK. The first half of the tutorial introduces higher-order network analysis with pathpy. The second half of the tutorial will cover higher-order cluster detection with the flow compression algorithm InfoMap. Extensive material, including slides, data, assignments, and solutions to live coding sessions is available at the tutorial website.

Lecture in ETH Zürich Symposium

In April 2018, Ingo Scholtes gave a lecture on higher-order network models in the symposium on networks, Time and Causality at ETH Zürich. A video recording of the symposium is available below.

Lecture at Northeastern University, Boston

In November 2017, Ingo Scholtes gave a lecture on multi-order network analysis at the Network Science Institute of Northeastern University in Boston, MA, USA. A video recording of the lecture is available below.
